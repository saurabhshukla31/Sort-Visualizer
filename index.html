Sorting Algorithms

Sorting algorithms arrange a set of data into a specific order. Computer science relies heavily on sorting for various tasks. There are two main types: logarithmic (complexity proportional to log(n)) and quadratic (complexity proportional to n^2). Algorithms are evaluated in terms of time complexity (speed) and space complexity (memory usage). Examples include Quick sort (logarithmic, O(n × log n)) and Bubble sort (quadratic, O(n^2)). Sorting algorithms help in understanding computational efficiency.

Space and time complexity can also be further subdivided into 3 different cases: best case, average case and worst case.

Sorting algorithms can be difficult to understand and it's easy to get confused. We believe visualizing sorting algorithms can be a great way to better understand their functioning while having fun!
